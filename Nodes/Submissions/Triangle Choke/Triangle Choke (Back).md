# Overview
The <u>Rear Triangle Choke</u> is the variation of the Triangle Choke from the Back.

![Rear Triangle Choke](https://www.bjjee.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/Triangle-Choke-from-the-back.jpg)
# Setup
- [Back](obsidian://open?vault=Obsidian-BJJ-Notes&file=Nodes%2FPositions%2FBack): Using a Kimura grip, bring both of your arms to one side. Then torque it, so your opponent can’t turn in. Hook your top foot at their elbow to isolate their head and arm. 


If you can’t break the grip for an arm from back.
Topm foot at elbow and other foot on top, press and pull until the arm is free, swtich hips and lock locks on arm side. squeeze legs

wrist lock

If you lock legs on other side of arm, americana
pin arm to you and bridge.
# Core Mechanics
- **Arm control**: By pinning your opponent’s arm to you, you limit their movement and escape.
- **Shin position**: Your shin should be positioned across their neck/chin. If it’s not, use your locking leg, while holding on to the strangle leg with your arm, to push on their to position yourself up.
- **Knee position**: With one knee at their carotid artery, point your other knee into them. This presses their shoulder into their artery.
- **Finishing mechanics**: Hamstring curl both legs.
