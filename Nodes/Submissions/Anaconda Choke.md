# Overview
The <u>Anaconda Choke</u> is a head and arm choke where your opponent’s arm is trapped with their head. This is applied by placing your arm under the opponent’s neck as you connect it to your bicep on the other arm, with the exit point of your arm on the outside of the opponent’s armpit.

![Anaconda Choke](https://cdn.evolve-mma.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/06/anaconda-choke-bjj-.jpg)
# Setup
- [Back](obsidian://open?vault=Obsidian-BJJ-Notes&file=Nodes%2FPositions%2FBack): If your opponent passes your strangle arm to the other side, the likelihood of you bringing the arm back is very low. The keys are grabbing your own knee, extracting the bottom hook, hiking your hip up, pointing the top knee down, front rolling, and catching your hand in their arm pit ([1](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/RWgTzhb2HUY)). You can also roll to the side ([2](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/r60ycrPzbpw)). This will not work if they’ve trapped your legs.
