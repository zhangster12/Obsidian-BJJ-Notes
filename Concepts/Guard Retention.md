# Overview
<u>Guard Retention</u> is the the crucial defensive skill of preventing your opponent from passing your legs to establish a dominant position
## Subconcepts
- [[Frames]]: Frames are defensive structural barriers created using your bones, typically knees, elbows, and forearms, to maintain space, prevent pressure, and recover guard.
- [[Kuzushi]]: Kuzushi is the Japanese martial arts principle of "breaking the balance" or "unbalancing" an opponent.
# Core Mechanics
- **Legs in front**: Constantly realign your hips to face the passer, preventing them from achieving an angle and passing around your legs.
- **Elbow-knee connection**: Maintain a tight, connected posture. Do not let your elbows drift away from your knees, which creates space for passes.
